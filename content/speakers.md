## Speaker Bios

**Michael C. Frank** (Stanford, confirmed) is the Benjamin Scott Crocker Professor of Human Biology at Stanford University and Director of the Symbolic Systems Program. He studies children's language acquisition and cross-cultural development using large-scale datasets.

**Uri Hasson** (Princeton, confirmed) is a Professor in the Psychology Department and the Neuroscience Institute at Princeton University. His research investigates how the brain processes information in the world and interacts with the environment, with a specific focus on natural language processing and human face-to-face communication.

**Kristen Grauman** (UT Austin, confirmed) is a Professor of Computer Science at UT Austin and a former Director at Meta's FAIR lab. Her research in computer vision and machine learning focuses on video understanding and embodied perception.

**Freda Shi** (U Waterloo, confirmed) is an Assistant Professor in the David R. Cheriton School of Computer Science at the University of Waterloo and a Faculty Member at the Vector Institute. Her research interests are in computational linguistics, natural language processing, and cognitive science, as well as related aspects of machine learning.

**Cordelia Schmid** (Inria, unconfirmed) is a Research Director at Inria and holds a joint appointment with Google Research. Her research focuses on computer vision, specialized in image matching, learning visual models, and fundamental video and image retrieval technologies.
